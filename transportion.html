Tourist place

Salto del Ángel, in Venezuela, is considered the highest waterfall in the world, with a drop of more than 800 meters. It was discovered in 1933, when aviator James Angel was flying over the Venezuelan jungle and, since then, it has gone from being a secret of the indigenous Pemón people to becoming a true icon of the country.

Tourist place of Brazil

Corcovado Hill, 710 meters above sea level, Christ the Redeemer is today one of the greatest symbols of Rio de Janeiro and Brazil and one of the most visited monuments in the world. A postcard that, in addition to being impressive in itself, offers one of the most beautiful views of the Marvelous City.
